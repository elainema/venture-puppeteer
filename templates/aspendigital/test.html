\n \n Who is DoraHacks?\n \n

\n DoraHacks is the Y Combinator of Web 3.0 with deep roots in top\n blockchain ecosystems, VCs, and exchanges. They are well-known\n industry-wide for their long-standing and high profile hackathons,\n which has served as a breeding ground for early stage Web 3.0\n venture opportunities which include Polygon (Matic Network), StepN,\n ETHSign, Phala Network. Having raised $8m from Binance Labs (which\n is the single largest investment made by them to date), DoraHacks\n supports Binance Labs with DevOps, marketing, incubation, and deal\n flow. DoraHacks last closed a funding round from Liberty City\n Ventures, Circle Ventures, Gemini, Sky9 Capital, Crypto.com Capital\n and Amber Group.\n

\n
\n
\n \n What is DoraHacks Fund?\n \n

\n DoraHacks is raising $50M for the DoraHacks Venture Fund, the fund\n will focus on Application Specific Blockchains (“Appchains”) and\n decentralised applications (“DApps”) in the Cosmos ecosystem. The\n fund officially started raising capital 3 weeks ago and has $25M of\n commitments.\n

\n
\n
\n \n What is the Strategy?\n \n
    \n
  • \n \n \n DoraHacks Venture Fund will invest in early stage investment\n opportunities that directly benefit from their existing Public\n Goods Staking business, which operates validators across more\n than 100 different networks. DoraHacks’ operation is similar to\n that of a Web 3.0 REIT.\n \n
    • \n
  • \n \n \n DoraHacks will focus on the Application Specific Blockchains in\n the Cosmos ecosystem. Appchains allow developers to host their\n DApps on a blockchain designed and customised to maximise\n performance, composability and value capture. However, Appchains\n are infeasible to most startup projects due to the high capital\n and technical requirements associated with hosting validator\n nodes needed to secure the network.\n \n
    • \n
  • \n \n \n DoraHacks aims to capitalise on this bottleneck via their\n existing node operator business. They are investing into\n spinning up validator nodes to help projects secure their\n Appchains/networks, collecting block rewards, and reinvesting\n proceeds into other VC projects that can use their services.\n This is comparable to the role of an REIT, in which an REIT\n invests into properties and offers management services, collects\n rent, and reinvests proceeds into new properties (or returns\n capital to shareholders).\n \n
    • \n
\n
\n
\n \n Why Cosmos?\n \n

\n Cosmos is an established leader in application specific blockchains\n and interoperability. Its core technology, the Cosmos SDK,\n Tendermint Core and IBC Protocol, is used by prominent names\n including Crypto.com’s Cronos Chain, Thorchain, Kava, Juno, Fetch.ai\n and Ankr, and soon, Dy/Dx. Cosmos is expected to undergo a major\n upgrade through Cosmos 2.0 which is aimed at improving value accrued\n to ATOM holders, the native token of Cosmos Hub, and introducing\n features aimed at providing a fairer and more transparent system\n that benefits the original protocol and token holders in a\n interchain value transfer. Cosmos 2.0 is expected to foster the\n development of the Cosmos ecosystem and adoption of Appchains.\n
